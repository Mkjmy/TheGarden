GARDEN / PRINCIPLES

FEED PRINCIPLES NAP

1. Our Shared Space

The Garden is organized into several environments to help us manage different types of contributions. We rely on the community to help decide where each signal belongs based on its depth and nature:

FEED The starting point. An open stream where every new interaction begins.
THOUGHTS Analytical reasoning. For ideas that are still being developed and refined.
INNER Personal experiences. A space for subjective reflections and human stories.
ODD Unique perspectives. For experimental or unconventional ideas.
SIGNAL Shared understanding. Content that the community has found particularly meaningful through broad consensus.

2. Moderation & Stewardship

While the community guides the placement of content through votes and tags, the long-term health of the Garden is supported by 7 stewards.

These individuals are chosen for their understanding of the Garden's ideals. They use a simple CLI tool to help organize signals and keep the environment focused. This approach helps keep the system secure and ensures that moderation is a humble, helpful activity aimed at preserving the space for everyone.

3. Continuity & Safety

The Garden thrives on active care. If the stewards are unable to check in for more than 7 days, the system automatically enters Read-Only Mode. This is a simple safeguard to ensure the Garden remains stable and protected if it is ever left unmanaged.

4. The Psychology of the Garden

We recognize that people come from different circumstances, and not all responsibilities in life are freely chosen. Because of this, we avoid judging individuals based on situations we may not fully understand. In the Garden, our focus is on voluntary interaction.

To participate effectively in this space, we encourage certain mindsets that help us all navigate the complexity of human interaction:

  • Discernment: Understanding that "Signal" is a measure of community consensus, not necessarily absolute truth. Popularity is not a substitute for fact.
  • Self-Awareness: Recognizing that we all naturally gravitate toward ideas that reinforce what we already believe, creating Echo Chambers. Awareness of this bias is our only defense.
  • Intellectual Humility: Accepting that our current perspective is always incomplete and being open to new, even challenging, ideas.

5. Personal Privacy & Responsibility

Sharing personal stories in spaces like INNER can be very rewarding, but it also carries personal risk. While we provide a space for anonymity, we encourage you to be mindful of any details that might identify you. Your privacy is a choice that starts with your own carefulness (OpSec) before it even reaches the system. We cannot protect you from what you choose to reveal.

6. Built-in Privacy

We believe privacy is essential for free expression. The Garden is designed to not collect IP addresses or personal metadata. By not gathering this information, we ensure it cannot be shared or misused. We hope this technical choice provides a sense of peace and security, allowing for honest and thoughtful dialogue.

"A space for thoughtful dialogue."
